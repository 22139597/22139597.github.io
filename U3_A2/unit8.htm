UNIT 8

In this unit, I learned about mobile apps and how they are used in daily life for things like navigation, entertainment, and communication. I explored the features of different apps and how they are designed and built. I used MIT App Inventor to design, develop, and test my own mobile app. I combined code snippets with ready-made and original assets like buttons and sounds. After finishing my app, I got feedback and reviewed it to find ways to improve it. This unit helped me understand how apps are made and improved my skills in app development.

More information

During this unit, I explored how mobile apps are used and created. I learned about the key features of mobile apps and used MIT App Inventor to design and develop multiple apps, here is three of them:

  • An alphabet learning app for children
  • A drawing color palette app
  • A xylophone notes app

These were the learning aims for this unit:

  • A understand the characteristics and uses of mobile apps
  • B design a mobile app
  • C develop and test a mobile app
  • D review the finished mobile app.
A-

I looked at two different mobile apps and explained what they are meant to do and what features they have. I talked about how each app works, what it does well, and what could be improved. This helped me understand the different types of apps and how they are used.

I tested each app, gathered feedback, and reviewed ways to improve them. This helped me understand the full process of mobile app development.

B-

I designed an app to help young children aged 3 to 7 learn the alphabet. The app was made to be bright, fun, and easy to use. It helps children learn the shapes of letters and what they sound like. I made sure the design was simple so that young users could enjoy using it without needing help.

C-

I created the app using different screens and made sure it was easy to use and worked as planned. I used some ready-made code and also wrote some of my own to match my design. I tested the app to check that everything worked, found any problems, and fixed them.

D-

I looked at my final app and checked that it was good for the age group and did what it was meant to do. I explained how testing and feedback helped me improve the app. I also said why I made some changes from my original plan and how they made the app better.
